# Evaluator license model

> **DRAFT FOR LEGAL REVIEW. NOT A LICENSE. DO NOT USE OR SIGN.**

No rights are granted by this engineering draft. Counsel must replace all bracketed placeholders,
confirm applicable law, and approve a final agreement before any evaluator receives software or
access under these proposed terms.

- Owner: `[OWNER LEGAL NAME AND ADDRESS]`
- Named evaluator: `[EVALUATOR LEGAL NAME AND ADDRESS]`
- Evaluation purpose: `[SPECIFIC NON-PRODUCTION PURPOSE]`
- Approved repository, package, or environment: `[IDENTIFIER]`
- Review window: `[START UTC]` through `[END UTC]`

## Proposed limited grant

The final agreement may grant only the named evaluator a limited, revocable, non-exclusive,
non-transferable, non-sublicensable right to access and internally evaluate the identified version
during the review window for the stated purpose. The final grant should not include production
use, commercial use, target testing, redistribution, publication, or rights to private operational
material.

## Proposed restrictions

The final agreement should prohibit:

- copying or redistribution beyond one approved evaluation environment;
- sublicensing, assignment, public mirroring, or shared credentials;
- production, managed-service, or customer use;
- security testing of any target without separate written authorization and technical scope;
- bypassing policy, authorization, redaction, evidence, or human-review controls;
- reverse engineering, decompilation, or circumvention only to the extent lawful and enforceable;
- benchmarking or public claims without written approval;
- removal of copyright, proprietary, attribution, or security notices; and
- use of private profiles, targets, accounts, correspondence, evidence, findings, or reports.

## Confidentiality and data handling

The final agreement should identify confidential information, permitted recipients, handling
controls, incident notification, compelled-disclosure process, exclusions, and survival period.
It should prohibit personal data and real assessment material unless a separately reviewed data
processing arrangement permits them.

The evaluator should keep materials in an approved encrypted environment, apply least privilege,
avoid external model or telemetry services unless approved, and report suspected loss promptly.

## Retention, return, and deletion

The final agreement should state exact retention periods and require return or verified deletion
at the earliest of review completion, termination, or owner request. It should address backups,
logs, derived notes, evidence, and written deletion confirmation.

## Feedback and intellectual property

The final agreement should define ownership of pre-existing rights, evaluation feedback,
suggestions, independently developed material, contributions, and any required assignment or
license. It should not assume that feedback ownership or contributor rights transfer
automatically.

## Support and security

The final agreement should identify whether support, updates, response times, maintenance,
security notices, or remediation are provided. Unless the final agreement states otherwise,
evaluation access should not imply production support or service-level commitments.

## Term, suspension, and termination

The final agreement should define the fixed term, termination rights, immediate suspension for
safety or confidentiality risk, post-termination obligations, and surviving terms. Authorization
to access a repository must end when the agreement or review window ends.

## Warranty, liability, and compliance

Counsel must draft warranty disclaimers, liability limits, indemnity if any, export and sanctions
terms, governing law, venue, notices, order of precedence, severability, and entire-agreement
language. Engineering documentation must not substitute for these terms.

## Approval record required before use

A final evaluator agreement remains blocked until it has:

- a legal-review identifier and approval date;
- final party names, version, purpose, and review window;
- a repository-owner signature and evaluator signature;
- a data-handling and retention decision;
- a support and security-contact decision; and
- a recorded distribution package digest.
